What is Skin Lesion?

Skin lesion is a type of cancer that develops from the skin cells. There are several types of skin cancer, with the most common ones being:

Basal Cell Carcinoma (BCC):

This is the most common type of skin cancer. It typically appears as a shiny, pink or red bump, or as a scaly patch that may be brown or flesh-colored. BCC usually grows slowly and rarely spreads to other parts of the body.

Squamous Cell Carcinoma (SCC):

SCC is the second most common type of skin cancer. It often appears as a firm, red nodule or a flat lesion with a scaly or crusty surface. SCC can grow and spread if not treated early.

Melanoma:

Melanoma is less common but more dangerous than basal cell and squamous cell skin cancers. It can develop from moles or appear as a new dark spot on the skin. Melanoma can spread quickly to other parts of the body if not detected and treated early.
